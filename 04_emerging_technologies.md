#  Internet of Things(IoT)

## What is IoT?
<p>A network of physical objects, including cars, appliances, and other items, that are equipped with sensors, software, and network connectivity so they can gather and exchange data is known as the Internet of Things (IoT).</p>

## What is the purpose of IoT?
<p>Connecting a wide range of things and gadgets to the internet is the primary goal of the Internet of Things. These gadgets can gather, exchange, and evaluate data in real time because to their connectivity. IoT also makes remote monitoring and control possible, allowing users to oversee their systems from any location in the world.

Establishing a smooth network of linked devices is one of the Internet of Things' most notable goals. This connectivity makes it easier to integrate systems and apps that may be used to automate processes, track performance, and streamline operations in a variety of industries, all of which eventually result in increased productivity and efficiency.</p>

## How does Internet of Things impact our lives?

### The Role of the IoT in IT industry

<p>IoT raises security standards by using networked devices to efficiently monitor and protect buildings. A complete security ecosystem is produced by the cooperation of smart sensors, cameras, and other IoT-enabled devices. These gadgets are capable of instantly identifying and reacting to a range of physical risks, including environmental dangers, suspicious activity, and illegal access. IoT systems can start preventive actions and send out instant notifications thanks to automation and advanced analytics. IoT-enabled access control systems improve the security of restricted areas by enabling dynamic modifications to authentication protocols and permissions. </p>

### The Role of the IoT in Education

<p>One essential instrument for increasing accessibility, interactivity, and collaboration in education is the Internet of Things. It can let students and teachers communicate in real time online. The classroom has been extended into the digital realm.

Learning is no longer hampered by physical distance. Even with the restoration of in-person instruction during the epidemic, the Internet of Things continues to play a vital role in education. From the concept to the prototype stage, it can facilitate collaborative group projects and make classroom learning more engaging and dynamic. </p>
